The smart world is set to enhance everyday things with abilities of sensation, communication, computation and intelligence so that many tasks and processes could be simplified, efficient, and enjoyable. It consists of numerous "smart things" that can be endowed with different levels/forms of intelligence and be connected together for a network level of intelligence. At the same time, the fairness and ethics in utilizing such an intelligence is of extreme importance. Research on ubiquitous and trustworthy smart world is an emerging research field covering many interdisciplinary areas that benefits humanity and will have significant societal impacts.

The IEEE Smart World Congress (SWC) originated from the 2005 Workshop on Ubiquitous Smart Worlds (USW, Taipei) and the 2005 Symposium on Ubiquitous Intelligence and Smart World (UISW, Nagasaki). SWC’s 2026 in Italy at the University of Calabria aims to provide a high-profile, leading-edge platform for researchers and engineers to exchange and explore state-of-art advances and innovations in graceful integrations of Cyber, Physical and Social Worlds with Ubiquitous Intelligence.

The topics of interest include (but are not limited to) the following:
  • Smart Wearables
  • Sustainable and Green AI
  • Digital Twin and IoT
  • Edge Intelligence
  • Privacy Computing and Data Security
  • Cognitive Environments and Metaverse
  • AI and Machine Learning
  • Gen AI-driven Educational Systems
  • Ubiquitous Intelligence and Computing
  • Smart Networks
  • Smart Applications (Smart Health, Smart Vehicles, Smart Manufacturing, etc.)

Types of Submissions

The conference welcomes high-quality, original research contributions that advance the scientific and technological foundations of the smart world. Authors are invited to submit their work in one of the following categories:

Full Papers (6 pages)

Complete research studies containing significant findings, rigorous methodology, and comprehensive experimental or theoretical results.

Short Papers (4 pages)

Concise contributions reporting promising early results, innovative ideas, or ongoing work that is expected to stimulate discussion and further development.

Poster & Demo Papers (2 pages)

Visual or interactive presentations highlighting prototypes, systems, applications, or preliminary research results. Demonstrations are encouraged to showcase practical innovations and emerging technologies.

Important: All submissions must be original, not previously published, and not under review elsewhere.

Call for Proposals of Special Sessions or Workshops

The IEEE SWC 2026 and its co-located conferences invite proposals for Special Sessions and Workshops that complement the main conference program. We seek innovative and focused sessions on emerging topics related to smart world technologies and their applications.

Proposal Guidelines

Please include the following information in your proposal:

  1. Title: Full title of the proposed special session or workshop
  2. Organizers: Names, affiliations, and contact information of the organizers
  3. Type: Specify if it's a Special Session or Workshop
  4. Duration: The number of hours or Half-day or Full-day
  5. Abstract: A brief description of the session/workshop (100-200 words)
  6. Topics: List of key topics to be covered

Submission Process

Submit your proposal as a Word file to the special session /workshop chair ?? at (??) by [Date], 2026 and the decision on Acceptance will be notified by [Date], 2026.

IEEE SWC 2026 Special Sessions and Workshops Information and Responsibilities

  • Session organizers /workshops are responsible for inviting 5 to 6 prospective authors (academics, researchers and professionals known in the field for their acknowledged work) to submit paper contributions. The invited authors should be active in the research topics addressed in the session.
  • Session /Workshop organizers are also responsible for organizing and carrying out the peer-review process either on an extended abstract or the full paper (it is up to them).
  • It is also the responsibility of the session /workshop organizers to set up any intermediate deadlines with the authors in a way that would ensure that the camera-ready version of all session papers reach the Conference Organizers on time for inclusion in the Conference Proceedings.
