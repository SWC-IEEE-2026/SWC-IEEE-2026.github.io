PhD Forum & PhD in ICT Day

The IEEE SWC 2026 PhD Forum, held jointly with the PhD in ICT Day, provides PhD students with a dedicated platform to present their ongoing research through a poster session. The Forum is organized within the framework of the PhD Program in ICT of the Department of Computer, Modeling, Electronics and Systems Engineering (DIMES) at the University of Calabria.

During a dedicated poster session, PhD students will have the opportunity to discuss their research with leading experts from academia and industry, receive constructive feedback, and build valuable connections within the research community.

Eligibility

The PhD Forum is open to PhD students enrolled in the 39th, 40th, and 41st cycle of the PhD in ICT program. Participants are invited to submit an extended abstract describing their research work.

Submission Guidelines

Submissions must consist of a 2-page paper formatted according to the official IEEE conference paper template (double-column, 10pt font).

Papers should describe the student's ongoing research and include the following key elements:

Required Paper Content
  • Motivation & Problem Statement
  • Research Methodology & Approach
  • Preliminary Results (if available)
  • Expected Key Contributions

Submission Policy: By submitting an abstract, authors agree that at least one author will register and present their poster in person at the IEEE SWC 2026 conference.

Paper Submission Portal

All papers must be uploaded via the Sparcly platform by the submission deadline.

Submit Paper via Sparcly

Poster Session & Awards

Accepted papers will be presented during a dedicated poster session at the congress. A jury of experts will evaluate the posters and award a Best Poster Award. Detailed formatting and dimension guidelines for the posters will be provided to authors upon acceptance.

Registration & Academic Credits (CFU)

Participants in the PhD Forum are eligible to register using the student registration fee. This registration grants full access to all IEEE SWC 2026 events, including 4 tutorials, 6 keynotes, 6 co-located conferences, 10 workshops, and 5 special sessions.

Academic Credits: Active participation in the PhD Forum and attendance at the related conference activities grants 3 CFU for PhD students of the DIMES ICT program.
